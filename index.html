Unit 3: Cellular Energetics

12-16%
Respiration
  1. Glycolysis: cytoplasm; ATP/NADH.
  2. Pyruvate oxidation and Krebs: mitochondria; CO2, NADH, FADH2.
  3. ETC: oxygen final electron acceptor; proton gradient powers ATP synthase.
Photosynthesis
  1. Light reactions: thylakoids; ATP, NADPH, O2.
  2. Calvin cycle: stroma; CO2 fixation into sugars.
Plants photosynthesize and perform cellular respiration.

Unit 4: Cell Communication & Cell Cycle

10-15%
Cell Signaling

Reception to transduction to response. Signal amplification can create large cellular responses from small signals.

Cell Cycle

G1 to S to G2 to M. Checkpoints, cyclins, and CDKs regulate division. Cancer results from loss of regulation.

Unit 5: Heredity

8-11%
Meiosis & Variation
  • Crossing over, independent assortment, and random fertilization generate variation.
  • Linked genes do not assort independently unless crossing over separates them.
Inheritance

Know dominance, incomplete dominance, codominance, sex-linkage, pedigree reasoning, and probability rules.

Unit 6: Gene Expression & Regulation

12-16%
Central Dogma

DNA to RNA to protein. Transcription makes RNA; translation uses codons to build polypeptides.

Regulation & Biotechnology
  • lac operon inducible; trp operon repressible.
  • PCR amplifies DNA, gel electrophoresis separates DNA, CRISPR edits DNA.

Unit 7: Natural Selection

13-20%
Evolution

Evolution is change in allele frequencies in a population over generations. Individuals do not evolve.

Hardy-Weinberg
p + q = 1
p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1

Use as a null model. Conditions include no selection, mutation, migration, drift, and random mating.

Units 8-9: Ecology, Biodiversity & Evolutionary Relationships

Ecology
  • Exponential vs logistic growth; carrying capacity limits populations.
  • Energy flows; matter cycles.
  • Interactions: competition, predation, mutualism, commensalism, parasitism.
Biodiversity
  • Nodes on cladograms represent common ancestors.
  • Shared derived traits define clades.
  • Speciation depends on reproductive isolation.

Quantitative Practice

Hardy-Weinberg
p + q = 1
p^2 + 2pq + q^2 = 1
Practice: 9% show a recessive trait. Find q, p, and heterozygotes.
q^2 = 0.09, q = 0.30, p = 0.70, 2pq = 0.42 = 42%.
Chi-Square
chi-square = sum[(O-E)^2/E]
Practice: Expected 3:1 in 100 offspring. Observed 80 and 20. Calculate chi-square.
Expected 75 and 25. chi-square = 25/75 + 25/25 = 1.33.
Graph Rates
rate = change in y / change in x

Use slope to calculate rates from graphs and always interpret biologically.

Final Exam Strategy

Common Mistakes

  • Individuals do not evolve; populations evolve.
  • Fitness means reproductive success.
  • Enzymes do not change equilibrium or delta G.
  • Plants perform cellular respiration.
  • Use units and cite data.

High-Scoring Stems

  • The data support the claim because...
  • As ___ increases, ___ changes because...
  • This change would increase fitness by...
  • Water moves from ___ to ___ because...
